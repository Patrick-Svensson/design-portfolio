Bhromaon A coding challenge to recreate a Figma desktop design and make it fully responsive with HTML and SCSS.

The Objective

Initially, it was a coding exercise included in the Front-End Development course at Sundsgårdens Folkhögskola.

The objective was to create a website, based on a Figma desktop wireframe, using HTML and CSS/Sass within a time limit of twelve hours. It had to be fully responsive on all devices from 320px screen size as well as cross-browser functional. It was up to the student to decide how the design would render in screen widths other than the wireframe's 1770px.

Result & Reflections

I did not manage to complete the entire design during the set time limit. When the time was up, I had finished the top three sections and the footer but had only the background images in place for the remaining sections.

Retrospectively, it might have been wiser to start coding the basics of the whole design and gradually work toward the details rather than finish each section at a time.

Later, I decided to finish the website as pixel accurately as I could manage.

Details & Decisions

Paying attention to details is what makes the work stand out from the crowd. Such as making sure the person in the background image remains well inside the picture frame as the image diminishes with smaller screens.

At the time, time was short, which made me choose a navigation component from Bootstrap. It was a quick and simple solution to implement a collapsable navbar without writing JavaScript. For good user experience, I let the navbar scroll along and stick to the top edge of the screen.

Responsiveness & Accessibility

I struggled a bit to get these two sections to render nicely on tablet and mobile. Getting the two offset pictures in the right place and lining up the icons with the arc lines png image was harder than it looked. It would probably be better to make the icons and the arc lines into one intact SVG.

Since the bottom section lacks a heading, I implemented a hidden h2 to improve the accessibility and SEO.
