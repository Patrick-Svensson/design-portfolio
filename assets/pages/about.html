Sometimes, fate desides to intervene. I got nudged into changing my career and grabbing the opportunity of learning new skills.

I'm Patrick Svensson, and to keep the osteoarthritis in my hands at bay, I chose to switch industries and take the opportunity to find a new playground. It so happened that I found myself hooked on UX/UI design, where I can still use the creativity and analytical skills I have practised as a boatbuilder and as a bar manager to seek clever solutions to problems, but in a digital context.

My partner came up with the idea, and it seemed like a good one, of trying out coding and aiming for web development. So, in the fall of 2020, we both got enrolled in a Web Development Bootcamp.

I found coding great fun and challenging, but what truly got me hooked was UX/UI Design. Since then, I have spent all my time and energy honing my UX/UI design skills, and my ambition is to use those skills to improve people's daily interactions and experiences with digital design.

I live on a traditional sailing boat, with an interior I carefully designed and built to both aesthetically tone with the correct era and beauty of the vessel as well as suit our needs as a live-a-board. The boat is moored up on the east coast of Sweden not far from Stockholm. Here, I enjoy the Baltic Sea, and cooking delicious vegetarian food together with my partner Helena and the ship's cat Mina.
